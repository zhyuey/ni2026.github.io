About the Workshop

The Neuromorphic Intelligence Workshop brings together researchers, engineers, and practitioners working on brain-inspired computing systems and their applications. This workshop aims to showcase cutting-edge research in neuromorphic computing, spiking neural networks, and their real-world applications.

Neuromorphic computing represents a paradigm shift from traditional von Neumann architectures to brain-inspired computing systems that can process information with unprecedented efficiency and adaptability. This workshop provides a platform for researchers to present their latest findings, discuss challenges, and explore future directions in this rapidly evolving field.

Topics of Interest

Neuromorphic Hardware

Novel hardware architectures, memristive devices, and brain-inspired computing platforms

Spiking Neural Networks

Learning algorithms, network architectures, and training methodologies for SNNs

Algorithms & Software

Interdisciplinary Research

Paper Submission

Submission Guidelines

  • Papers should be 6-8 pages in length (excluding references)
  • Use the standard IEEE conference format
  • Submissions should be in PDF format
  • Double-blind review process
  • All submissions will be peer-reviewed by at least 2 reviewers

Submission Topics

We welcome submissions on all aspects of neuromorphic intelligence, including but not limited to:

  • Novel neuromorphic hardware architectures and implementations
  • Spiking neural network algorithms and learning methods
  • Applications in robotics, computer vision, and signal processing
  • Software frameworks and development tools
  • Benchmarking and performance evaluation
  • Interdisciplinary research connecting neuroscience and computing
